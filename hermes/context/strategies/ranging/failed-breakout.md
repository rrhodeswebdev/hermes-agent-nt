# Playbook (Ranging) — Failed Breakout

**Regime fit**: ranging markets ONLY. The trap-reversal trade: when a breakout from
the range **fails**, the trapped traders' exit fuels a fast move back across the box.

## The idea

Most range breakouts fail. When price pokes beyond an edge, sucks in breakout traders,
and then **closes back inside the box**, those traders are trapped — their stops are
your tailwind. This is the highest-energy setup ranges offer, and the legitimate way
to re-engage an edge after a probe (where a plain range-fade would be too early). It
is the mirror image of strategies/trending/breakout-continuation.md: same event,
opposite resolution — delta quality is what separates the two.

## Entry conditions (failed upside breakout → short; mirror for downside → long)

ALL must be true:

1. **Regime**: an established range (edges respected ≥ 2 times, box ≥ ~3 × ATR).
2. **The false break**: price trades **beyond** the box high — far enough to matter
   (≳ a few ticks to ~0.5 × ATR beyond), pulling breakout buyers in.
3. **The failure trigger**: a bar **closes back inside** the box. The close back
   inside IS the signal — wick beyond, body back in. Until that close, it is a
   breakout, not a failure; never anticipate.
4. **Flow shows the trap** (order-flow.md): the push beyond the edge ran on weak/
   divergent delta, or absorption ate it at the highs. If the breakout carried strong,
   clean delta and only paused — that is continuation risk, not a trap; veto.
5. **Room**: ≥ ~1R back into the box from entry.

## Bracket

- **Stop**: just beyond the false-break extreme (≈ 1.0–1.5 × ATR total). Above that
  extreme the "failure" has un-failed; you must be out.
- **Target**: the middle of the box minimum; the opposite edge when the box width
  pays — trap moves tend to travel further and faster than plain fades.

## Management

- The move should work **quickly** — trapped traders bail fast. If price stalls back
  at the entry edge for several bars instead of rotating away, the energy thesis is
  failing: take the early exit.
- **Invalidation**: a fresh close back outside the edge → exit immediately.
- Never widen the stop. One attempt per false break — no re-entries if stopped.

## Skip it when

- The "break" never closed back inside (still a live breakout).
- Delta on the break was strong and stayed one-way (real breakout pausing, not a trap).
- The same edge has already false-broken twice this session — third pushes usually go
  (edges weaken with each test).
- The box is too narrow (< ~3 × ATR) for the rotation to pay ≥ 1R.
